Google Chrome's New Protection Against Cookie-Stealing

1712246400

Google recently announced a new defense against cookie-stealing attacks called Device Bound Session Credentials (DBSC). The project is being publicly developed on GitHub.

TL;DR

DBSC attempts to bind session cookies to the device it was originally for. Effectively making the cookie useless, in theory, to attackers. It would use a device-bound key pair, where the private key is stored on the device and a public key is sent to the server. The server then uses the public key to verify that the device has possession of the key.

Taking a Deeper Dive

The Problem with Cookies

Right now, cookies can be exfiltrated from a device with malware or social engineering and be used for hijacking the user's session. If you have kid's that had their Roblox account hacked, they were likely a victim of of this common attack.

Your best protection against cookie-stealing is to be safe on the internet by not downloading unknown files or sending your browser's data to anyone online (even if they promise free Robux).
